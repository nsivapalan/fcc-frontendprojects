Bernie Sanders

Quick facts about Bernie:

  • Born: September 8, 1941 in Brooklyn, New York.
  • Actively fought in the Civil Rights Moment, performing the first Chicago sit-in in 1962 along with 32 other students, fighting against discrimination and segregation.
  • Marched alongside Martin Luther King, Jr. in 1963 at the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom.
  • Voted against the 2003 invasion of Iraq.
  • Ran for POTUS 2016 as a relatively unknown candidate and came within ~500 delegates of becoming the Democratic nominee in a tightly-contested race. Rejecting traditional fundraising via Super PACs, Sanders' campaign broke the record for most number of donations, averaging $27 per donor.
  • Started a political revolution, continuing his fight as a politican against the "big" banks, corrupt politicians, and income inequality.
